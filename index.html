About me

I am a PhD student with the Machine Intelligence Lab at the Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge, supervised by Roberto Cipolla. My research interests include 3D reconstruction and shape representation.

My PhD is sponsored by Trya Srl, a company looking to revolutionise the digital shoe shopping experience. My current research is in the field of 3D shape reconstruction - specifically, I am looking at methods of recovering foot shape from images captured on a mobile phone.

My latest research includes:

  • FIND: A generative, implicit model for feet. Try out our web demo below!
  • SynFoot: Large scale synthetic foot data useful for reconstruction tasks.
  • FOUND: Surface normal predction (trained from synthetic data), followed by 3D shape reconstruction fusing multiview normals and uncertainty.
FIND model demo Surface normal prediction on in-the-wild images from FOUND. The predictor was only trained on synthetic renders of 8 foot scans!

I used to be a coxswain for Cambridge University Boat Club. I was Men's President of the club last year, in our clean sweep win over Oxford. I coxed Goldie, the men's reserve crew, between 2021 and 2023.
